# Name Continuo Disposition Honestly

Vellum will classify every continuo result as a complete realization, a complete realization with a separate bass, or a Continuo Reduction. It will determine whether the target can sound every authoritative foundation bass event at its reviewed pitch. Harmonic implication, pitch-class coverage, and upper-octave doubling do not count as sounding the Continuo Foundation.

## Considered options

- Treat any realization of the figures as complete
- Silently octave-displace or omit an incapable target's bass
- Require an explicit disposition and preserve every unsounded bass event in lineage and audit evidence

## Consequences

A capable target such as piano sounds the foundation itself. When a re-entrant five-course guitar cannot reach the foundation, Guided Start offers a named ensemble target with an explicit separate bass instrument. The guitar's generated upper voices are independently fitted to playable target positions; the separate bass events retain their source pitch, timing, semantic role, and instrument identity. Notation and Audio Preview label both instruments rather than attributing the bass to the guitar.

Arrangement Search also generates a Continuo Reduction candidate. Its source foundation events remain linked, its unsounded bass events receive hard `continuo.foundation_unsounded` findings, and the candidate is rejected under Faithful Reduction. It cannot win merely because its upper voices imply the same harmony. Selecting such a reduction will require the later explicit Policy Exception or less restrictive Preservation Policy workflow.

An incapable target without a separate-bass decision blocks with a targeted choice instead of guessing. Every selected result stores a Continuo Disposition with its label, sounded and unsounded foundation event IDs, and bass instrument. The workbench discloses this beside the Preservation Audit.
