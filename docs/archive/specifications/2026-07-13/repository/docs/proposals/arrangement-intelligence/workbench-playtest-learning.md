# Workbench, Playtest, and Reviewed Learning

Status: Draft subordinate proposal

## Default Owner journey

1. Upload or import source.
2. Choose Target Configurations, Notation Layouts, Preservation Policy, and Performance Brief.
3. Review only consequential uncertainty.
4. Review a proportional plain-language Arrangement Plan.
5. Receive independent target realizations.
6. Open the selected artifact automatically with notation, Audio Preview, readiness, and major compromises.
7. Select, loop, hear, edit, request alternatives, or record a playtest.
8. Adopt changes as exact new versions and narrow commitments.

Progressive disclosure is mandatory. Solver metadata never becomes a default question. Every summary claim links to complete evidence, and expert mode changes presentation rather than canonical semantics.

## Canonical-layer classification

Before applying a natural-language or direct request, Vellum classifies whether the Owner intends:

- Score Transcription correction;
- Analysis Claim correction;
- Arrangement Plan revision;
- Arrangement Score edit;
- Performance Interpretation change;
- Editorial or Family Commitment;
- Policy Exception;
- Personal Default Candidate; or
- explanatory feedback with no mutation.

The model may propose classification but cannot silently commit it. Source and Analysis corrections follow their own lineage and staleness paths rather than becoming arrangement constraints.

## Score-anchored interaction

Selection context contains exact workspace, score version, target, policy, event, measure, source, plan, lineage, and finding identities.

The workbench supports:

- multi-event selection and range selection;
- synchronized source, arrangement, audit, plan, and playback navigation;
- temporary loop and audition;
- batch manual edits with stale-response protection;
- plan-aware passage alternatives;
- target-local and Family Commitment promotion;
- direct repair suggestions;
- new-version adoption and branch comparison; and
- recovery after correction, provider, validation, or rendering failure.

Manual edits are Owner-authored candidates. Validation reruns hard mechanics, plan realization, complete Transformation Report, and Preservation Audit. Only intentionally protected dimensions become Commitments.

## Arrangement and performance layering

- Notated pitches, rhythms, voices, ornaments, and required tablature positions belong to Arrangement Score.
- Hidden physical fingering plans remain candidate evaluation evidence when not engraved.
- Right-hand fingering, unnotated arpeggiation, ornament realization, articulation, inequality, rubato, and other playback-only choices belong to Performance Interpretation when canonically retained.
- Unrepresented performer execution remains disclosed as unevaluated.

Literal Audio Preview projects Arrangement Score. Interpretive playback is separately versioned and selectable.

## Owner Playtest

An Owner Playtest identifies:

- exact Arrangement Score or candidate;
- exact passage and Playback Occurrences;
- Instrument Instance Configuration;
- Performance Brief;
- actual tempo or practice context when relevant;
- whether notation, listening, or physical playing was used;
- outcome and confidence;
- structured observations; and
- free rationale.

Default outcomes:

- comfortable;
- playable with practice;
- marginal;
- unplayable;
- unclear or unmusical;
- historically questionable;
- notation problem; or
- not tested.

Observations include reach, shift reliability, held-note conflict, right-hand difficulty, damping, voice clarity, cadence, source identity, and notation.

The Owner Playtest can support personal adoption, rejection, correction, Commitment, ergonomic-profile proposal, Calibration Candidate, or regression fixture nomination. It cannot change universal mechanics or Historical Knowledge.

## Human evidence roles

Human Evaluation records one explicit role:

- Owner usability reviewer;
- target-instrument player;
- historical-practice specialist;
- editor or engraver;
- independent musical listener; or
- other declared role.

Suites specify which roles can support each dimension. Owner judgment is authoritative for personal adoption and personal calibration. Target-player evidence supports physical execution within its context. Historical claims require cited evidence and, where human review gates them, an appropriately scoped specialist. One reviewer cannot silently stand in for every role.

## Reviewer protocol

Human protocols define:

- anchored rubric examples;
- reviewer role and qualifications;
- heard versus physically played evidence;
- candidate-order randomization and balancing;
- blindness to candidate identity where practical;
- duplicate or calibration cases when required;
- confidence and conflict-of-interest disclosure;
- minimum evidence for a comparative conclusion;
- disagreement and adjudication policy; and
- privacy and consent.

“Anonymized” means blinded to candidate identity and implementation where practical; notation or musical style may still reveal information. One preference is stored as one scoped judgment, not automatically as a regression.

## Reviewed learning

The system accumulates:

- adopted and rejected candidates;
- Playtests and Human Evaluations;
- manual edits and accepted repairs;
- source and Analysis corrections;
- evaluator predictions that disagreed with reality;
- recurring target-local and family-wide choices; and
- explicit Owner usefulness markers.

It may propose:

- Personal Default Candidates;
- Owner Ergonomic Profile candidates;
- Knowledge Candidates with citation requirements;
- evaluator-calibration examples;
- Golden Fixture candidates; and
- minimal counterexamples.

No proposal changes behavior before the relevant existing review boundary accepts it.

## Calibration isolation

Evidence is assigned one role for a particular evaluator version:

- calibration or fitting;
- development and debugging;
- held-out evaluation; or
- post-deployment monitoring.

The same example cannot both fit an evaluator and prove its generalization. Moving evidence between roles creates a new dataset version and invalidates incompatible comparisons. Held-out fixture expectations are unavailable to generation and fitting paths.

Evaluator updates record fitting inputs, held-out inputs, target scope, known limitations, and historical disagreement. They create new evaluations rather than rewriting prior evidence.

## Privacy and retention

- Playtest media is optional and local-first.
- Recordings require explicit purpose, consent, and retention scope.
- Provider-bound summaries are redacted and separately authorized.
- Explicit Owner usefulness replaces hidden activity telemetry.
- Private workspace evidence cannot enter repository fixtures automatically.
- Fixture nomination exports only reviewed, licensed, deliberately selected content.
