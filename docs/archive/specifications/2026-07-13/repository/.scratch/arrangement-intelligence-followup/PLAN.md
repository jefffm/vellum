# Arrangement Intelligence idiom and coherence follow-up

Status: ready-for-agent

## Outcome

Turn the accepted three-target prototype baseline into genuinely idiomatic phrase-level realization for five-course baroque guitar, thirteen-course baroque lute, and six-string classical guitar. The three targets are coequal. Shared contracts must support each target without flattening its distinct technique, notation, texture, or evaluation semantics. Historically and editorially scoped behavior must be compiled from reviewed Knowledge Packs rather than embedded as unexplained instrument-wide solver constants.

## Boundary inherited from the prototype closeout

The predecessor wave established source lineage, Analysis, Briefs and Plans, exact Instrument Instances, bounded search, notation, playback, workbench interaction, and evaluation infrastructure. It did not establish calibrated physical playability, historical authority, or coherent multi-voice realization. The Owner accepted that boundary on 2026-07-13 and supplied three exact counterexamples:

- baroque guitar: a nominally playable Greensleeves result that is neither idiomatic punteado nor valid mixed-style alfabeto writing;
- baroque lute: measure 3 uses `f/b`, an unacceptable fret-1-to-fret-5 span on the Owner's 690 mm lute despite closer equivalent realizations; and
- classical guitar: the Principal Voice survives but the source's 59-note bass becomes four isolated events rather than a coherent target bass.

## Execution order

1. Source identity, citation segments, Knowledge Pack profiles, and the knowledge-to-compiler applicability bridge
2. Shared intended-technique, target-voice, ergonomic, and counterexample contracts
3. Five-course baroque-guitar punteado, rasgueado, mixed-style, and alfabeto phrase realization
4. Thirteen-course-lute scale-aware stopped-course and diapason ergonomics
5. Six-string classical-guitar Target Voice Plans and joint polyphonic realization
6. Cross-target output evaluation, workbench alternatives, and reviewed acceptance

The first slice follows [Instrument Knowledge Source Ingestion](../../docs/proposals/instrument-knowledge-source-ingestion.md) and supplies reviewed, scoped inputs rather than attempting to finish historical research once and for all. Instrument mechanics and initial failing counterexamples may be developed while extraction matures, but no historical heuristic is considered complete until its cited pack profile and applicability are executable. Slices 3–5 are coequal sibling realizations of slice 2 and may proceed independently once its contracts are stable. Slice 6 depends on all three.

## Completion boundary

- Each exact Owner counterexample is a failing output-level regression before its fix and a passing regression afterward.
- Search plans musical phrases and intended target texture before choosing physical positions.
- Instrument mechanics, performer ergonomics, intended technique, and historical evidence remain separate typed inputs.
- Search identity records the exact Knowledge Pack versions and digests that supplied historical or editorial behavior.
- Every historically grounded candidate consequence links to its applicable profile, claims, examples, and source segments; unresolved or conflicting practice remains visible.
- Candidate reports expose rejected alternatives, binding constraints, transformations, and unknowns.
- Playback can isolate every planned target voice or technique layer.
- Evaluation grades the generated notes, rhythms, positions, technique events, engraving, and playback rather than trusting declared invariant IDs.
- A real PDF-upload browser path produces all three sibling outputs and exposes meaningful alternatives for the reviewed regions.
