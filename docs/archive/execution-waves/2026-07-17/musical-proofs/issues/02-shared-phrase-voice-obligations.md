# 02 — Shared phrase and voice obligations

Status: completed

Type: AFK

Blocked by: 01

## What to build

Carry the baseline source's voice identity, activity, rests, phrase contour, cadence, harmonic
function, and preservation obligations through analysis, planning, candidate search, score,
audit, UI explanation, and evaluation. The narrow vertical must support a Principal Voice and
at least one coherent subordinate voice without assuming that every source has a permanent
melody.

## Acceptance criteria

- [x] Source Voice Graph evidence distinguishes voice identity from notation carrier and pitch height.
- [x] Target Voice, Harmonic, and Relationship Plans express phrase-level obligations used by search.
- [x] A selected candidate preserves the Principal Voice under Faithful Reduction or fails explicitly.
- [x] A planned subordinate voice cannot disappear or jump without a failing audit; material outside the target plan is explicitly omitted.
- [x] The arrangement view's existing Transformation Report explains retained, transformed, omitted, and generated material at score locations.
- [x] Mutations of phrase timing, cadence, or voice continuity fail the focused evaluation.

## Result

The Old 100th path now persists a Source Voice Graph and target-local phrase obligations. Search
uses those obligations rather than treating pitch-class coverage as voice identity. The
classical-guitar sibling proves Principal Voice plus an independently audited bass skeleton;
unaligned source attacks are explicitly omitted instead of being falsely linked. The narrower
historical-target plans preserve the Principal Voice and explicitly defer subordinate idiom to
tracers 03 and 04, where technique and mechanics can generate honest target-specific material.

## Gates

Base gates plus affected `eval:*` suites.
