# Arrangement Intelligence Requirement Ledger

Status: Initial executable-specification inventory

Authoritative sources: every Markdown file under `docs/proposals/arrangement-intelligence/`

Execution plan: the approved 45-slice dependency-ordered tracer plan

This ledger decomposes the draft Arrangement Intelligence specification into stable, atomic requirements. A requirement is not complete merely because its row says `implemented`: completion also requires the cited evidence to exist at the final commit, remain compatible with the current requirement and evaluator semantics, and pass the final completion audit.

## Conventions

### Status

- `implemented`: current-state inspection found direct implementation and relevant verification.
- `partial`: current behavior covers a proper subset or uses weaker semantics.
- `absent`: the named contract or behavior is not present in the current repository.
- `unverified`: implementation may exist, but no sufficiently scoped evidence was inspected during this initial inventory.

Initial statuses are reconnaissance, not completion claims. Any row may move to `implemented` only when its required evidence is linked in the eventual Completion Manifest.

### Acceptance gates

- `G0 — Contract`: stable IDs, ownership, and evidence obligations exist.
- `G1 — Operational foundation`: the local runtime, trust boundaries, persistence, and review path are safe enough to support browser work.
- `G2 — First tracer`: one immutable source completes the narrow Source Truth → Plan → Search → Evaluation → adoption loop.
- `G3 — Intelligence foundation`: general Source Truth, Analysis, Planning, constraint, and instrument contracts exist.
- `G4 — Three-target realization`: baroque guitar, 13-course lute, and classical guitar each use the new realization path.
- `G5 — Evaluation and learning`: playtest, comparison, calibration, CI, retention, and reporting contracts operate.
- `G6 — Three-target parity`: all three priority targets satisfy equivalent end-to-end product semantics and required human gates.
- `G7 — Cross-domain validation`: continuo and imitative domains validate shared contracts without being flattened.
- `G8 — Architecture acceptance`: focused ADRs and migration boundaries are accepted.
- `G9 — Final completion`: the machine completion audit finds no missing, stale, incompatible, failed, or awaiting mandatory evidence.

### Machine-evidence families

- `M01 Domain`: schema validation, type-level exhaustiveness, serialization/store round-trip, migration, and immutable-lineage tests.
- `M02 API`: typed route contract, safe error, authorization, and service integration tests.
- `M03 Browser`: real-browser E2E with observable DOM, recovery, navigation, adoption, and artifact assertions.
- `M04 Source`: recorded import/OMR fixtures, consequence classification, correction, resume, and purpose-authorization tests.
- `M05 Analysis/plan`: reviewed fixture assertions for roles, relationships, plan decisions, conflicts, and staleness.
- `M06 Instrument`: exact sounding-set, course, tuning, notation-identity, technique-applicability, and invalidation tests.
- `M07 Search`: reference-search differential, property, replay, cancellation, checkpoint, completeness, and diagnostic tests.
- `M08 Preservation`: exact retained-event/relationship checks, Transformation Report, Preservation Audit, and mutation sensitivity.
- `M09 Render`: LilyPond compilation, semantic notation assertions, focused visual regression, accessibility, and hostile-content sanitization.
- `M10 Playback`: canonical sounding-event identity, occurrence, traversal, duration, part, and literal/interpretive separation tests.
- `M11 Evaluation`: case/manifest/run/card schema, immutable resolution, comparison, baseline, materiality, and promotion tests.
- `M12 Mutation`: known-bad transformations are detected with the required scope, authority, and presentation.
- `M13 Persistence`: concurrency, revision, crash/restart, orphan repair, collision, retention, GC, and private-deletion tests.
- `M14 Security`: loopback/CORS/auth boundary, upload/subprocess bounds, active-content sanitization, and information-disclosure tests.
- `M15 Model`: recorded-provider deterministic contracts plus explicitly opt-in live-evidence metadata tests.
- `M16 CI`: impact-map tests and required `typecheck`, full tests, formatting, specification verification, and applicable evaluation suites.
- `M17 Corpus`: licensed fixture digest/provenance checks and target-specific Golden workflow runs.
- `M18 Statistics`: uncertainty, sampling, aggregation, reviewer-evidence threshold, and materiality policy tests.
- `M19 Documentation`: static cross-reference, terminology, requirement-to-tracer, ADR, and Completion Manifest verification.

Each row names the relevant family and the exact behavior that the evidence must prove; a green family-level command alone is insufficient.

### Human-evidence roles

- `H0 None`: no subjective or authority-bearing human judgment is required beyond code review.
- `H1 Owner`: Owner confirms intent, usefulness, workflow, privacy, or adoption under the exact cited context.
- `H2 Musicology`: scoped source/analysis/plan review by a qualified musicological reviewer.
- `H3 Player`: physical playtest by the intended target-instrument player under the exact Performance Brief and Instrument Instance.
- `H4 Historical`: appropriately scoped historical-practice review with cited evidence.
- `H5 Editorial`: notation/engraving/editorial review of exact rendered artifacts.
- `H6 Listener`: independent recognition or musical-coherence review under a declared protocol.
- `H7 Security/privacy`: explicit trust-boundary, consent, or private-deletion review.
- `H8 Baseline`: authorized baseline promotion or comparison adjudication with rationale.
- `H9 Architecture`: Owner acceptance of the focused ADR and migration boundary.
- `H10 Final`: Owner acceptance of complete product usefulness and outstanding disclosed tradeoffs.

## Ledger completeness rules

1. Every requirement row has exactly eight pipe-delimited fields: ID, atomic requirement, source section, owner tracer, gate, machine evidence, human evidence, and status.
2. IDs are permanent. If a requirement changes materially, retain the old ID with a superseded disposition and create a new ID.
3. Tracer issues may own many requirements, but every requirement has one accountable primary owner tracer or an explicit ordered owner set.
4. Human evidence is required only where the row says so. The only HITL execution gates are tracers 41–44; earlier machine tracers may define protocols and schemas but may not block on the Owner being present.
5. A row may be marked complete only when its exact evidence is linked in the Completion Manifest and compatible with the final implementation, inputs, and evaluator semantics.
6. The machine pre-HITL audit at tracer 40 must treat required human evidence as `awaiting_scheduled_HITL`, not as a machine pass.
7. The final closure at tracer 45 must reject `awaiting_scheduled_HITL` and every other non-complete mandatory disposition.
