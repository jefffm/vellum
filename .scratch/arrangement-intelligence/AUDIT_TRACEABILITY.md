# Arrangement Intelligence Audit Traceability

Status: Active execution ledger

Source audit: `TECH_DEBT_AUDIT.md`

Scope: Findings F001-F047 mapped to the approved Arrangement Intelligence tracer plan

## Purpose

This ledger prevents the Arrangement Intelligence program from declaring completion while a known audit finding is merely discussed, indirectly tested, or lost inside a broader tracer. Every finding has at least one owning tracer, a concrete verification obligation, and an explicit set of allowed final dispositions.

The audit's evidence label describes the evidence available when the audit was written. It does **not** mean that the finding is resolved. At creation time every row below is open; no post-audit implementation or verification evidence has yet been attached here.

## Disposition rules

Exactly one final disposition must be recorded for every finding:

- **fixed+verified**: the current implementation removes the defect or supplies the missing capability, and proportionate automated and, where required, human evidence proves the claim at the final commit;
- **disproven**: a focused reproduction, reachable-path analysis, and regression test show that the asserted behavior or risk is not present. Failure to reproduce, a passing unrelated test, or code inspection alone is insufficient;
- **superseded by accepted decision**: `CONTEXT.md` or an accepted ADR explicitly changes or resolves the product/policy premise behind the finding. A draft proposal is insufficient;
- **Owner-accepted residual**: the remaining behavior, exposure, or limitation is documented precisely and the Owner explicitly accepts it against exact current evidence. This is never an automatic fallback and cannot be inferred from inactivity.

Additional rules:

- An observed defect cannot be marked `disproven` merely because it no longer reproduces after an untracked change; that is `fixed+verified` once the causal fix and regression evidence are identified.
- A capability gap normally closes only as `fixed+verified` or `superseded by accepted decision`.
- A risk may close as `disproven` only with exploit- or race-oriented evidence that exercises the suspected boundary.
- Human-in-the-loop evidence may be deferred to the final HITL tracers, but the row remains open until that evidence is attached.
- Evidence must identify the exact implementation commit, fixture/input, configuration, evaluator/test version, command or protocol, result, and retained artifact where applicable.
- A later change that invalidates cited evidence reopens the row. The Completion Manifest must reject open, stale, unmapped, or ambiguously disposed rows.
