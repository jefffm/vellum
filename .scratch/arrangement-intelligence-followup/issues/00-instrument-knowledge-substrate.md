# Instrument-knowledge source ingestion, reviewed pack profiles, and compiler applicability

Status: ready-for-agent

Type: AFK

## Tracer

Import one reviewed public-domain reference for each target domain, extract one exact cited segment from each, promote or author narrowly scoped reviewed knowledge, compile applicable profiles into one passage-level Arrangement Plan, and record the exact pack identities in search and evaluation. Use the seed library and extraction cases in [Instrument Knowledge Source Ingestion](../../../docs/proposals/instrument-knowledge-source-ingestion.md).

The tracer proves the end-to-end knowledge boundary. It does not claim to complete research for any instrument.

## Acceptance criteria

- Owner References distinguish Work, Edition, Exemplar, Digital Asset, and cited Source Segment identities.
- Underlying-work rights, scan-provider terms, and private-library access remain separate assertions.
- A streamed PDF or image upload is content-addressed, deduplicated, rendered into a page atlas, and never committed to Git by default.
- Printed locator, scan or canvas locator, bounding box or musical range, original transcription, optional normalized translation, extraction method, and confidence survive review.
- Extracted material is classified as prescription, worked example, descriptive repertoire observation, modern editorial synthesis, Vellum heuristic, or performer observation.
- OCR, OMR, tablature recognition, and model inference create candidates only; none can promote itself.
- Knowledge Packs contain applicable profiles, examples, counterexamples, validation guidance, and executable compiler mappings in addition to claim IDs.
- Conflicting claims remain separately scoped alternatives and can produce an explicit Plan ambiguity.
- The knowledge-to-compiler bridge produces typed Plan alternatives, candidate generators, constraints, and evaluator inputs with claim and segment provenance.
- Search execution identity includes exact pack versions and digests; reports expose which consequences they caused.
- A later source can corroborate, narrow, contradict, or supersede a claim through a new reviewed version without rewriting prior evidence.

## Seed demonstrations

- Mace PDF page 105 / printed page 75 preserves `a`, `/a`, `//a`, `///a`, `4`, `5` and refuses to invent a thirteenth-course convention.
- Sanz distinguishes rasgueado, punteado, and context-dependent fourth-finger use rather than compiling a universal three-finger prohibition.
- Carulli aligns at least one multi-part source texture with its guitar reduction and yields reviewed voice-transformation examples.
- One Weiss manuscript page yields cited descriptive tablature observations without being promoted as a universal rule.
- Sor's 1830 first edition and Harrison's 1896 rewritten edition remain different authorities despite sharing an underlying work lineage.

## Honest boundary

Private modern methods such as Satoh and future Owner-provided Serdoura scans may support local modern-editorial candidates and practical comparison. Their full content is not exported or bundled, and they never masquerade as primary historical testimony.
